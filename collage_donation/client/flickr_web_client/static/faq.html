Frequently Asked Questions

What do I need?

To use Collage Photo Donation, you need:
  • A recent Web browser. Firefox 3, Chrome, Safari and Internet Exporer 8 should all work, among others.
  • Adobe Flash Player. Version 9 and later should work.
  • A Flickr Pro account. If you don't have an account, you can create one.
  • JavaScript must be enabled.

Why do I need a Flickr Pro account?

For Collage to work, users of Flickr must be able to access the full-size, original images you upload to your Flickr account. Unfortunately, only Pro account holders can access their original images.

How do I upload photos?

To choose photos to donate to Collage, simply click "Select images to upload". This will open a file chooser where you can select your photos. You may select multiple photos at a time. Once you choose the photos, they will be uploaded. Afterward, a thumbnail of each photo will be shown.

To support uploading many photos at once, you must have Adobe Flash Player installed. This feature is provided by SWFUpload.

Can I donate photos that I previously uploaded to Flickr?

You can use Collage Photo Donation to upload photos that are not already stored on your Flickr account. It is not possible for Collage Photo Donation to modify photos that you have already uploaded using another upload tool.

What is the expiration time?

The expiration time is the maximum amount of time we will hold your photos on our servers before sending them to Flickr.

When you upload your photos to Collage Photo Donation, we hold them on our servers before uploading them to Flickr. During this time, we search for censored content that can be hidden in your photos. The longer you allow us to hold your photos, the greater the chance that we can fill them with censored content.

Due to Flickr Terms of Service, we will never hold your photos longer than 24 hours.

The expiration time has nothing to do with deletion of your photos from Flickr. If you want to delete your photos from Flickr, you may do that directly through Flickr's Web site

Why do I need to tag my photos?

Collage users locate photos containing censored content using the tags on your photos. If you do not tag your photos, then Collage users cannot find the censored content stored inside the photos.

It is important that you do not remove the tags on your photos after they have been uploaded to Flickr, because this will compromise the ability to locate the censored content hidden inside your photos.
